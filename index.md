## Welcome to the Archive

# Remembering Old Roblox

*A Digital Archive of Discontinued Platform Features*

Welcome to **Remembering Old Roblox**, a digital archive that documents features, systems, and interfaces that have disappeared from Roblox over time.

While Roblox is often understood as a game platform, this project approaches it as a platform made up of many interconnected systems. These systems shaped how users communicated, participated, built identities, earned currency, and navigated the platform. As Roblox evolved, many of these features were redesigned, replaced, or removed entirely.

This archive preserves evidence of those earlier systems through screenshots, archived webpages, community discussions, wiki documentation, and platform announcements. Rather than recreating Old Roblox, the project examines how the platform functioned during earlier periods and explores what is lost when digital platforms change.

Many online platforms are treated as if they exist in a continuous present. However, platforms are constantly evolving. Features disappear, interfaces are redesigned, communities migrate, and forms of participation become impossible. These changes often leave behind only fragments of evidence scattered across archived websites, screenshots, forum discussions, and community memories.

By bringing these materials together, this archive preserves traces of Roblox's earlier history and highlights how platform design can shape culture, participation, and user experience.

## Explore the Archive

The archive is organized into five major categories that represent different aspects of Roblox's historical development.

### Economy

This section explores the Tickets (Tix) economy, currency systems, and changes to Roblox's virtual marketplace. Artifacts document how users earned, exchanged, and interacted with currency before major economic changes were introduced.

### Access and Identity

This section focuses on guest accounts, avatar systems, onboarding processes, and other features that shaped how users entered and participated within the platform.

### Communication

This section documents Roblox forums and discussion spaces that once served as important community hubs. These artifacts explore how users communicated, shared feedback, and participated in platform culture.

### Interface History

This section preserves historical website designs, navigation systems, login pages, and other interface elements that demonstrate how Roblox's visual identity changed over time.

### Membership Systems

This section examines Builders Club, Turbo Builders Club, and Outrageous Builders Club memberships as systems of status, monetization, and platform participation.

## About the Collection

The archive currently contains **46 artifacts** gathered from publicly available historical materials, including archived webpages, screenshots, Roblox Wiki documentation, community discussions, blog posts, and platform announcements.

Each artifact includes metadata such as title, description, subject terms, source information, and dates. These metadata choices help connect individual artifacts to broader platform systems and themes, allowing users to explore relationships across the collection.

Remembering Old Roblox
This is a digital archive of early Roblox, focusing on features, systems, and design choices that have since been removed, redesigned, or replaced.

Rather than treating Roblox as a single continuous platform, this archive looks at it as something layered: a system that has changed its rules, interfaces, and identity over time.

What this archive collects: 
This project documents fragments of “Old Roblox,” including:

discontinued UI and interface designs
legacy economy systems and currencies
early social and communication features
older account and identity structures
community practices shaped by earlier platform constraints
Each item in the collection is treated as a trace of how the platform used to function, not just what it looked like.

Why this exists?
Digital platforms don’t stay still.

Features disappear. Systems get rewritten. Entire ways of interacting quietly fade out as platforms evolve.

This archive exists to preserve those shifts,  not as nostalgia alone, but as a way of understanding how platform design shapes culture, behavior, and memory.

How to use this site: 
You can browse the collection to explore individual artifacts from different versions of Roblox history.

Each entry represents a snapshot of a system, feature, or moment that has been altered or removed from the platform today.

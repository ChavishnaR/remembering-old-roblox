# Remembering Old Roblox  
### A Digital Archive of Discontinued Platform Features

---

## About the Archive

This project documents features, systems, and interfaces that have disappeared from Roblox over time.

While Roblox is often understood as a game platform, this archive approaches it as a network of interconnected systems. These systems shaped how users communicated, built identities, accessed the platform, participated in communities, and engaged with its economy.

As Roblox evolved, many of these systems were redesigned, replaced, or removed. This archive preserves fragments of those earlier configurations through screenshots, archived webpages, community discussions, wiki documentation, and platform announcements.

Rather than recreating “old Roblox,” the project examines how the platform functioned at different moments in time and what is lost when digital systems change.

---

## Platform as Infrastructure

Online platforms are often treated as continuous and stable. In reality, they are constantly shifting infrastructures.

Interfaces are redesigned. Economies are restructured. Communities migrate. Forms of participation disappear.

These changes leave behind fragmented traces across the web — archived pages, screenshots, forum posts, and community memory.

This archive brings those fragments together to show how platform design shapes culture, participation, and user experience.

## About the Collection

This archive currently contains 46 artifacts drawn from publicly available historical materials, including archived webpages, screenshots, wiki documentation, community discussions, blog posts, and platform announcements.

Each artifact includes metadata such as title, description, subject terms, source, and date. These metadata choices allow artifacts to be understood both individually and as part of broader platform systems.
